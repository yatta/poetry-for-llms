A simple page

With an invisible layer worth reading.

What this page is

This is a demonstration page for the Poetry for LLMs project. It looks simple because it is. The visible content is minimal by design.

The interesting part is what you can't see in a browser. View the source.

Three ideas

  • Your audience has an audience.
  • The invisible layer is read more often than the visible one.
  • Craft scales. Gaming doesn't.

What to do with this

Fork this page. Replace the content with your own. Write comments where the markup gives your language a second reading. Add structured data that tells the truth about what you do, not just what schema.org requires.

Then run the tool:

npx poetry-for-llms ./index.html
See what a machine reader encounters vs. what a human sees.

Why bother

A machine that deeply reads your site will represent your thinking to every human who asks. Not because you gamed a ranking. Because you gave it something worth reading.

The cost is a few hundred bytes per page. The return is a reader that carries your ideas into conversations you're not part of.
