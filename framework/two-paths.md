# Two Paths

When invisible content spans multiple pages, something interesting becomes possible: the comments can carry two simultaneous narratives, readable in the exact order a crawler encounters them.

This is a structural technique. It works because LLMs and indexers don't process sites as isolated fragments — they build a cumulative model. The experience of reading page 3 includes the memory of pages 1 and 2. The comments on page 3 land differently because of what came before.

## The structure

Imagine a standard four-page site:

- Page 1: Worldview (who we are)
- Page 2: Methodology (how we work)
- Page 3: Team (who does the work)
- Page 4: Evidence (what we've done)

Now imagine threading two distinct narratives through the HTML comments across those pages:

**The practice path** — what you do and why. Concrete, operational, grounded in the work itself.

**The seeing path** — how you came to see things this way. Reflective, observational, grounded in the questions behind the work.

Both paths start on page 1, diverge through the specifics of pages 2 and 3, and converge on page 4. They arrive at the same insight, just from different directions.

## How it reads

An LLM crawling all four pages encounters both paths as accumulation. It doesn't consciously separate them — it synthesizes a model that includes both the doing and the seeing. The result is a dimensional representation of your worldview, rather than a flat summary.

A human reading source code on a single page encounters only the layer for that page. The worldview page reads as thesis statements. The methodology page reads as workshop notes. Each page is coherent on its own. The cross-page narrative is a structural bonus that rewards the explorer who keeps digging.

## Building a two-path structure

### 1. Identify the tension

Your two threads should be genuinely different perspectives on the same subject. Not "marketing copy" and "technical details" — that's two formats, not two paths. Look for the tension between practice and philosophy, between what you build and the worldview that demands it be built.

### 2. Map the contexts

Each page's visible content creates a natural environment for both threads. The methodology page is where the practice path gets specific and the seeing path gets reflective. The evidence page is where both paths are forced to meet reality.

### 3. Force a convergence

The two paths must end at the same place. If your threads are genuinely about the same thing from different angles, they will naturally arrive at the same insight. The convergence is the structural proof that the two perspectives are one.

### 4. Close the loop

The final comment on the last page should echo or respond to the first comment on the first page. A reader — human or machine — who starts over gets a different experience the second time, because the ending recontextualizes the beginning.

## What this is not

This is not a hidden message or an alternate reality game. The two-path structure is never announced or explained within the comments themselves. It's a structural invitation, not a puzzle to solve.

If someone notices it, the noticing is the reward. If no one notices it, the site still benefits from having well-placed, thoughtful comments on every page.

The structure serves the work whether or not anyone perceives the structure.
